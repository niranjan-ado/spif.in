Know Suicide, No Suicide.

Anyone can prevent suicide by learning to recognize the warning signs and how to offer support.

1 Indian Every 3 Minutes

With over 164,000 suicides annually, India is the country with the most suicides globally. The World Health Organisation estimates this number could be even higher.

8 out of 10 People Show Signs

Suicide is the most preventable form of death. As a community, we can play a vital role by learning the verbal, non-verbal, and written warning signs.

Understanding Suicide

Suicide is a desperate attempt to escape suffering that feels unbearable. It rarely results from a single cause but rather when multiple life stressors create overwhelming feelings of hopelessness.

Mental Health

Mood, conduct, or personality disorders, often undiagnosed or untreated.

Social & Cultural

Isolation, stigma, and discrimination based on gender, sexuality, or caste.

Environmental

Access to lethal means, neglect, abuse, and personal or societal trauma.

Lifestyle

Substance abuse, chronic lack of sleep, and unhealthy body image issues.

Physical Health

Traumatic brain injury, chronic debilitating pain, or disease.

Precipitating Events

Traumatic changes in relationships or sudden distressing life events.

Warning Signs

Verbal Cues

  • Talking about wanting to die or kill themselves.
  • Expressing feelings of unbearable pain.
  • Saying things like "My family would be better off without me."
  • Mentioning they "won't be around much longer."

Behavioural Cues

  • Increased drug or alcohol abuse.
  • Sleeping too much or too little.
  • Isolating from friends and family.
  • Giving away prized possessions.
  • Unexplained anger or irritability.

Emotional Cues

  • Sudden and extreme mood swings.
  • Expressing hopelessness, helplessness, or worthlessness.
  • Feeling trapped or like a burden to others.
  • Loss of interest in activities once enjoyed.

Protective Factors

While risk factors exist, there are also many factors that offer a safety net. Suicide prevention seeks to reduce risks and increase these protections.

  • ✅ Strong social connections to friends and family.
  • ✅ Life skills such as emotional resilience and problem-solving.
  • ✅ A sense of purpose or meaning in life.
  • ✅ Cultural or religious beliefs that discourage suicide.
  • ✅ Access to quality mental health services and support.

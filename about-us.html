About SPIF

Who We Are

Founded in September 2017, Suicide Prevention India Foundation (SPIF) is a Section-8 not-for-profit that was incubated at IIM-Bangalore.

SPIF believes that suicide is the most preventable form of death. At the core of our approach is the WHO-recommended "Gatekeeper training" strategy. We have a tie-up with the QPR Institute, the world leader in suicide prevention training, to deliver this life-saving program which involves questioning, persuading, and referring a suicidal individual to professional help.

Our Mission

To create a community of organizations and individuals dedicated to suicide prevention through awareness, self-care, research, advocacy, and training.

How We Work

Core Focus

  • QPR Gatekeeper Training: Customised training for communities, schools, colleges, and workplaces.
  • Professional Training: Advanced courses for mental health professionals on triage assessment and counseling for suicidal behaviors.

Adjunct Focus

  • Awareness: Spreading knowledge through talks, workshops, social media, blogs, and media articles.
  • Research: Publishing original research to plug gaps in the understanding of the Indian mental health ecosystem.

Our Founder

Nelson Vinod Moses is a journalist who writes on mental health and social entrepreneurship. His work has appeared in Fortune, Forbes, The Times of India, and more.

The loss of his best friend to suicide made a profound impact on him. Over the years, he has been researching and understanding mental health and suicide prevention. Suicide Prevention India Foundation is the fruition of that journey.
